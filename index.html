About Me

If you’re reading this, then congratulations, you’ve already taken the first step to becoming a happier, healthier you – welcome to my website. My name is Ayla, that’s ‘Eh-la’ for my Canadian friends out there. I am an intern at the Robert Shad Naturopathic Clinic where I am currently working through my fourth year residency.


Why Naturopathic medicine? Despite common perception, there is an alternative, healthier solution to sustainable wellbeing. Naturopathy looks beyond bandage solutions and instead at what caused the problem in the first place. This is what excites me most about alternative health care.


My undergraduate degree is from the University of Saskatchewan where I studied Anatomy and Cell Biology. Spending time in this field has driven my passion for all things brain related. I’m excited to use my knowledge of science and health to work with patients to optimize their vitality. If you have any questions about Naturopathic Medicine, or what it can offer, please, send me an email. I also invite you to follow my journey in Naturopathic medicine by checking out or subscribing to my blog.

Clinic Information

The Robert Shad Naturopathic Clinic is the teaching clinic for the Canadian College of Naturopathic Medicine. As your intern, I will be collaborating with experienced Naturopathic Doctors to create a comprehensive and flexible therapeutic strategy specific for your health care needs. Through various modalities and an evidence-based approach, we work together to restore your bodies ability to function optimally as well as treat symptoms.

What to Expect

Our initial appointment will typically last one hour. In that time we will walk through your medical history and current concerns in addition to a partial physical examination.


As our clinic is a teaching clinic, it will be important for my supervisor and I to spend time in between appointments reviewing the literature and recent research related to your specific case. This means that we likely will not discuss specific treatment plans until the second and/or third appointment.


Follow-up appointments consisting of progress updates, providing on site physical modalities and preventative care can range from 45-60 minutes.
